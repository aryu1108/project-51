chameleon

  1. Chameleons are reptiles that are parts of the iguana suborder
  2. Changing skin color is an important part of communication among chameleons
  3. Most chameleon have a prehensile tail that they use to wrap around tree branches

camel

  1. They are two different species. Asian camels, known as Bactrian camels, have two humps, while Arabian camels, known as Dromedary camels, have one.
  2. Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  3. Wide feet and thick leathery pads help them to walk on the hot sand and keep them from sinking.

cicadas

  1. Cicadas are harmless.they do not bite or sting
  2. Most 17 Year Cicadas have red eyes, but they can also have white, gray, blue , or multi-colored eyes
  3. Cicadas need to be warm to sing and fly around, but they’re cold-blooded. Their dark skin absorbs the heat of the sun, which helps to warm them up.

panda

  1. An adult panda weight more than 45 kilos (100 pounds), and can be about 1.5 meters (5 feet) long!
  2. A 45-kilo adult (and pandas can reach 150 kg in captivity) spends as long as 14 hours eating. And it can eat 12 to 38 kilos of bamboo a day.
  3. “Giant panda” is actually just a nickname for a panda; they are no specifically larger than others.

pigeons

  1. The pigeons have been around humans for thousands of year.
  2. Pigeons can reach top speeds of up to 77.6mph for short periods and have been recorded flying as fast as 92.5 mph!
  3. Scientists have discovered that the common pigeon actually has an astonishingly good long-term memory.
